Fluidization of dense suspensions

Utilizing a blend of Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) and Discrete Element Method, simulations explore fluid injection into a dense suspension comprising liquid and particles. Employing the MFIX software, the study focuses on magma chambers to unveil insights into volcanic eruption mechanisms. These simulations yield fresh constraints crucial for understanding the genesis of volcanic eruptions.

Heat transfers in complex reservoirs

Utilizing a blend of Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) and Discrete Element Method, simulations explore the injection of a heated liquid into a cooler dense suspension, aiming to analyze heat transfer efficiency in complex reservoirs. The focus is on magma chambers, with the simulations conducted using the MFIX software.

Settling in suspensions

Modeling the settling behavior of a dense suspension within a viscous liquid by employing a combination of Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) and Discrete Element Method. The study aims to investigate the impact of intergranular lubrication, with a particular focus on its application to understand sedimentation processes. These simulations were conducted using the MFIX software.
