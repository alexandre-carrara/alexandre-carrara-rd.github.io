Magma reservoir dynamics

Fluidisation of dense suspensions

Simulations of the injection of fluid in a dense suspension (liquid and particles) using a combination of Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) and Discrete Element Method. The simulations were performed using the software MFIX. Application to the study of magma chambers. The simulations provided new constraints on how volcanic eruptions are generated

Fluidization regime. The color of the particles depends on the magnitude of the contact forces helping to highlight the force chains. The red color corresponds to the injected liquid.

Lateral spreading regime.

Study of the influence of Fluid flow on structures

Simulations of the flow of liquid around a 3D structure (liquid and particles) using the Finite Element Method (FEM). Application to olivine crystals. The simulations were performed using the software MigFlow. These simulations helped in constraining the aerodynamical properties of minerals

Simulation of the flow of liquid around a mineral. The simulations were used to calculate the force applied by the flow of fluid on the mineral. The color of the streamlines depends on the flow velocity

Thermal convection. The green curve indicates the interface between the hot and convecting fluid and the cold and motionless one.

Heat transfers in complex reservoirs

Simulations of the injection of a warmed liquid into a colder dense suspension to study the efficiency of heat transfers in complex reservoirs using a combination of Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) and Discrete Element Method. Application to magma chambers. The simulations were performed using the software MFIX

Settling of suspensions

Simulations of the settling of a dense suspension in a viscous liquid using a combination of Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) and Discrete Element Method. Application to the study of the influence of intergranular lubrication. The simulations were performed using the software MFIX
